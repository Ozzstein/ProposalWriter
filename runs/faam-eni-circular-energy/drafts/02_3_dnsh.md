# Section 2.3 — Minimum Requirements

## 2.3.1 Compliance with DNSH Technical Screening Criteria for Climate Change Mitigation

The primary economic activity of the FAAM-ENI Circular Energy project is the **manufacture of batteries** as defined under Section 3.4 of the EU Taxonomy Regulation (Regulation (EU) 2020/852) and its associated Climate Delegated Regulation (Commission Delegated Regulation (EU) 2021/2139).

**Regulatory position: No additional justification required.** Section 3.4 ("Manufacture of batteries") is listed in Table 2 of the Delegated Regulation as an economic activity for which **no technical screening criteria (TSC) exist for the climate change mitigation objective**, and therefore **no additional justification of alignment to DNSH for climate change mitigation is required** [CLM-022]. This classification applies to the entirety of LFP cathode active material production, which constitutes a critical upstream manufacturing step in the battery value chain. The project's scope — manufacturing LiFePO4 cathode active material from iron phosphate, lithium carbonate, and glucose precursors — falls squarely within the "Manufacture of batteries" activity definition.

This regulatory classification is confirmed in the Innovation Fund Application Guidance, which specifies that activities listed in Table 2 with the status "No TSC, Therefore Not Required" do not require applicants to provide additional evidence of DNSH compliance for climate change mitigation.

**Nevertheless, the project's intrinsic climate mitigation credentials are substantial.** While no TSC-based justification is mandated, the project design delivers significant climate benefits through two complementary mechanisms:

**(a) Progressive electrification of process heat.** The Brindisi plant achieves a 66.6% electrification rate at entry into operation, with 3,580 kWh/t delivered electrically and 189 m3/t of natural gas retained for process steps where full electrification is not yet technically feasible at scale. Electric roller hearth kilns achieve 95% thermal efficiency compared to 25-60% for gas-fired equivalents, representing a 1.6-3.8x thermal efficiency advantage [CLM-023]. The plant architecture is designed for progressive gas elimination (targeting >95% electrification by Year 10). As Italy's grid decarbonises under the NECP trajectory (from approximately 310 gCO2/kWh in 2025 to a projected 146 gCO2/kWh by 2030 and approximately 30-60 gCO2/kWh by 2035 under G7 commitments), the product carbon footprint advantage compounds over the plant's 20+ year operational life [CLM-026, CLM-030].

Electricity consumption during LFP battery manufacture is the single largest contributor to climate change impact, accounting for 39.7% of total Global Warming Potential [CLM-024]. By electrifying production and locating within the EU's rapidly decarbonising grid, the Brindisi plant directly addresses the highest-leverage emission reduction opportunity in the LFP supply chain.

**(b) Digital twin-driven process optimisation.** The integrated ISO 23247-compliant digital twin enables real-time process control, predictive quality monitoring, and energy optimisation across the production chain. Cross-industry evidence demonstrates that digital twins consistently deliver 10-50% scrap reduction and 5-15% energy savings in process industries [CLM-007]. Scrap reduction directly translates to avoided embedded emissions: every rejected batch represents wasted energy, raw materials, and associated upstream Scope 3 emissions. DT-optimised calcination temperature profiles, informed by the TwinHeat approach (demonstrated 10-15% energy reduction in industrial heat treatment furnaces [CLM-016]), further reduce per-tonne energy consumption and associated GHG emissions.

**Compliance confirmation:** The project complies with the DNSH principle for climate change mitigation. No TSC applies to the manufacture of batteries under Section 3.4, and the project design inherently advances climate mitigation through electrification and DT-optimised resource efficiency.

## 2.3.2 ETS Benchmark Emissions

The project produces lithium iron phosphate (LiFePO4) cathode active material — a specialty inorganic chemical product. Under Commission Implementing Regulation (EU) 2021/447 (setting EU ETS benchmark values for the 2021-2025 period, applicable at call opening on 4 December 2025), LFP CAM does not have a directly corresponding product benchmark.

The EU ETS product benchmark system covers 52 products across sectors including cement, steel, aluminium, and basic chemicals. LFP cathode active material — a high-purity electrochemical material produced at relatively low volumes compared to ETS benchmark commodities — is not among the listed benchmark products. The closest potentially applicable categories are:

- **Carbon black benchmark** (0.954 tCO2/t product): Not applicable. LFP CAM is not a carbon product; carbon coating constitutes only 1.0-1.8 wt% of the final product [CLM-009].
- **Chemicals heat benchmark** (6.84 GJ/tCO2): While the calcination step consumes thermal energy, LFP CAM production is fundamentally a materials synthesis process with complex reaction kinetics rather than a simple heat-driven commodity chemical process.

**Assessment:** LFP cathode active material production does not correspond to any EU ETS benchmark product category. The project's process emissions per unit of product are therefore not subject to ETS benchmark comparison requirements under this call.

LFP cathode active material does not have a direct EU ETS benchmark product under Commission Implementing Regulation (EU) 2021/447. The project falls under the 'Manufacturing of components for energy storage' category (ES), and per Section 1.3.4 of the GHG emission avoidance methodology, the manufacturing plant is outside the GHG system boundary. The GHG avoidance is calculated based on the use phase of the ESS systems in which the manufactured LFP CAM is deployed. Therefore, the ETS benchmark comparison is applied to the downstream ESS application rather than to the CAM manufacturing process itself.

## 2.3.3 Sustainability of Biomass

**Not applicable.** The FAAM-ENI Circular Energy project does not use biomass feedstocks in any stage of the LFP CAM production process. The three primary raw materials are:

1. **Iron phosphate (FePO4)** — inorganic mineral precursor
2. **Lithium carbonate (Li2CO3)** — inorganic mineral precursor
3. **Glucose (C6H12O6)** — used as a carbon source for the conductive carbon coating on LFP particles

While glucose is derived from agricultural sources, its function in the process is as a chemical precursor for carbon coating (pyrolysed at 600-700 C to form amorphous carbon) rather than as a biomass energy feedstock. Glucose constitutes a minor fraction of total input mass (carbon content target: 1.0-1.8 wt% in final product [CLM-009]) and is consumed in the chemical reaction, not combusted for energy generation. The sustainability requirements of Renewable Energy Directive 2018/2001 (Part A of Annex IX, or certified low ILUC-risk per Commission Delegated Regulation (EU) 2019/807) are therefore not applicable to this project.

No biomass is used for energy generation at the Brindisi facility. The majority of process heat is supplied by grid electricity through electric heating elements (resistance heating in roller hearth kilns and spray dryer systems), with limited natural gas use (189 m3/t LFP CAM) retained in the initial configuration for auxiliary heating. The progressive electrification strategy aims to eliminate residual gas use over time.

**Compliance confirmation:** The biomass sustainability criterion does not apply. No biomass feedstocks are used in LFP CAM production at the Brindisi facility.
