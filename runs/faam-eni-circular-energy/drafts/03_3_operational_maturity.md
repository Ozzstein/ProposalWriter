# 3.3 Operational Maturity

## 3.3a Overview

The FAAM-ENI Circular Energy project is currently at the pre-Final Investment Decision (pre-FID) stage of development. Key project development activities completed to date include: (i) selection and characterisation of the Brindisi brownfield site owned by Eni; (ii) definition of the 7-step LFP CAM production process and preliminary engineering design; (iii) development of the digital twin architecture specification based on ISO 23247 [CLM-008, SRC-007]; (iv) completion of preliminary market analysis confirming EU demand for domestic LFP CAM supply [CLM-001, CLM-003, SRC-023]; and (v) preparation of the present Innovation Fund proposal.

The implementation plan is designed to achieve **financial close within 24 months** and **entry into operation within 48 months** of grant agreement signature, targeting the positive scoring consideration for CLEAN-TECH-MANUFACTURING projects that demonstrate accelerated timelines. These targets are achievable given the mature state of the core manufacturing technology (solid-state LFP synthesis at TRL 7-8), the availability of the Brindisi brownfield site with existing industrial infrastructure, and the combined project execution capabilities of FIB (FAAM) and Eni [CLM-022].

**Reference**: Detailed implementation planning is supported by the mandatory Feasibility Study annex and the Business Plan annex.

### Phase 1: Grant Agreement to Financial Close (M0-M24)

This phase focuses on advancing project maturity from pre-FID to financial close through four parallel workstreams:

**Workstream 1 -- Detailed Engineering**: Progression from preliminary design to Front-End Engineering Design (FEED), including: finalisation of process flow diagrams and P&IDs; equipment specification and sizing (spray dryer, calcination furnace, jet mill, classifier, packaging line); site layout and building design for the Brindisi brownfield location; utility requirements (electricity supply, nitrogen generation, water treatment, HVAC); and integration of DT sensor and communication infrastructure into the engineering design.

**Workstream 2 -- Permitting**: Completion of Italian environmental and construction permitting processes (detailed in Section 3.3d). Early engagement with the Comune di Brindisi and the Regione Puglia environmental authorities has been initiated. Eni's established relationship with local permitting authorities, based on decades of industrial operations at the Brindisi site, is a significant de-risking factor.

**Workstream 3 -- Procurement and Supplier Qualification**: Identification, qualification, and contracting of critical equipment suppliers (furnace, spray dryer, milling systems, QC instruments, DCS/SCADA systems). Long-lead items (calcination furnace, spray dryer) will be ordered by M18 to support the M24 construction start.

**Workstream 4 -- Financing**: Finalisation of the project financing structure, equity commitments from FIB and Eni, debt facility negotiation, and execution of all financial close documentation. This workstream is described in detail in Section 3.2 (Financial Maturity).

### Phase 2: Financial Close to Entry into Operation (M24-M48)

**Construction** (M24-M36): Civil works and building construction on the prepared Brindisi brownfield site; installation of utilities, process equipment, and instrumentation; piping, electrical, and control system installation.

**DT Development and Integration** (M12-M42, parallel track): Development of physics-based models (CFD, PBM, reaction kinetics); training of surrogate models (PINNs); deployment of sensor network and OPC-UA communication infrastructure; integration of models with real-time data pipeline; initial calibration using commissioning batch data.

**Commissioning and Testing** (M42-M48): Mechanical completion and pre-commissioning checks; utility testing (electricity, N2, water); dry commissioning of individual equipment units; wet commissioning with process materials; production of qualification batches; DT calibration and validation against commissioning data; final CQA verification against battery-grade specifications; operational readiness assessment.

### Phase 3: Operational Monitoring (M48-M108)

**Year 1 (WP3, M48-M60)**: Production ramp-up from commissioning rate to nameplate capacity; DT calibration with production-scale data; process optimisation guided by DT insights; annual GHG emission avoidance report.

**Years 2-5 (WP4-WP7, M60-M108)**: Steady-state operation at nameplate capacity; continuous DT model improvement with accumulated production data; annual GHG monitoring and reporting; knowledge sharing activities.

### Strategy for Financial Close and Entry into Operation

The project's strategy to achieve the accelerated timelines (FC within 2 years, EiO within 4 years) is based on three pillars:

1. **Mature core technology**: Solid-state LFP synthesis is at TRL 7-8 with commercially available equipment. No fundamental technology development is required before construction -- the primary risk is process integration and optimisation, not technology feasibility [CLM-011, SRC-ED-009].

2. **Brownfield site advantage**: The Brindisi site is an existing Eni industrial facility with available land, utility connections (grid electricity, water), transport infrastructure (port and rail access), and established relationships with local authorities. This eliminates greenfield site development time (typically 6-12 months for site acquisition, access roads, utility connections).

3. **Strong sponsor commitment**: FIB (FAAM) and Eni bring complementary capabilities (manufacturing expertise and site/energy management, respectively) and the financial resources to advance project development in parallel with the Innovation Fund application process.

## 3.3d Permits, Rights, Licences, and Regulatory Procedures

### Permitting Framework

The Brindisi LFP CAM manufacturing plant is subject to the following Italian and EU permitting requirements:

**Environmental Impact Assessment (VIA -- Valutazione di Impatto Ambientale)**: Required under Italian Legislative Decree 152/2006 (Codice dell'Ambiente) for new industrial installations exceeding defined thresholds. The VIA procedure is administered by the Regione Puglia for regional-competence projects or by the Ministero dell'Ambiente e della Sicurezza Energetica (MASE) for national-competence projects. The typical VIA timeline is 12-18 months from application submission to decree issuance. Pre-consultation with the competent authority has been initiated.

**Integrated Environmental Authorisation (AIA -- Autorizzazione Integrata Ambientale)**: Required under the Italian transposition of the EU Industrial Emissions Directive (2010/75/EU) for industrial activities covered by Annex I. The AIA integrates atmospheric emissions, water discharge, waste management, and noise requirements into a single permit. The AIA process is typically conducted in parallel with or following the VIA, with a timeline of 6-12 months.

**Building Permits (Permesso di Costruire)**: Required from the Comune di Brindisi for the construction of industrial buildings and structures. Building permits are contingent on compliance with urban planning instruments (Piano Regolatore Generale) and the area's industrial zoning designation. The Brindisi site is located within an established industrial zone, which is compatible with manufacturing use.

### Brownfield Site Status

The Brindisi site is an existing Eni industrial facility. Eni has conducted site characterisation and, where required, remediation activities in accordance with Italian contaminated sites regulations (Title V, Part IV of Legislative Decree 152/2006). The site status is:

## 3.3e Project Management Team and Organisation

### Consortium Structure

The project is implemented by a two-partner consortium with clearly delineated roles:

**FIB (FAAM) -- Coordinator**
FIB S.p.A., operating under the FAAM brand, is an Italian battery manufacturer founded in 1974 (Monterubbiano, FM, Italy), a subsidiary of Seri Industrial S.p.A. With approximately 354 employees and over 50 years of experience in lead-acid and lithium-ion battery production for traction, industrial, storage, and military applications, FIB brings established manufacturing expertise and a commitment to green batteries with low environmental impact. FIB operates under ISO 9001, ISO 14001, and ISO 45001 management systems. FIB assumes the role of project coordinator with responsibility for:
- Overall project management and coordination
- LFP CAM process engineering and manufacturing operations
- Quality control and assurance
- Digital twin system specification and operation
- Knowledge sharing and dissemination

**Eni -- Co-Investigator**
Eni S.p.A. is a global energy company with extensive experience in industrial site management, process engineering, and energy supply. Eni contributes:
- Brindisi industrial site (land, existing infrastructure, utility connections)
- Process engineering support (leveraging Eni's refining and chemicals expertise)
- Energy supply management (grid electricity procurement, potential on-site renewable capacity)

### Governance Structure

The project governance comprises three levels:

1. **Steering Committee**: Strategic decision-making body comprising the FIB CEO (or delegate) and the Eni Project Director (or delegate). Meets quarterly or as needed for material decisions (scope changes, budget reallocation >10%, risk escalation). Decisions by consensus; escalation to respective corporate boards if needed.

3. **DT Working Group**: Specialist body comprising the DT Lead (FIB), DT modelling engineers, sensor integration specialists, and IT/OT infrastructure managers. Meets bi-weekly during development phases (M12-M48), monthly during operations. Responsible for: model development progress; data architecture decisions; DT-process integration testing.

### Quality Management and Health & Safety

The project will implement quality management in accordance with ISO 9001:2015 and, where applicable, IATF 16949 for automotive-grade battery materials. The quality management system covers: incoming material inspection; in-process quality monitoring (linked to the DT CQA framework); batch release testing; supplier quality management; and traceability (full batch genealogy from raw material lot to packaged product).

Health and safety management follows Italian workplace safety legislation (D.Lgs. 81/2008, Testo Unico sulla Sicurezza) and will be documented in a project-specific Health and Safety Plan. Key H&S considerations include: handling of fine powder (dust explosion prevention, respiratory protection); nitrogen atmosphere operations (asphyxiation prevention, O2 monitoring); high-temperature equipment (burn prevention, thermal insulation); and electrical safety for electrified process equipment.
