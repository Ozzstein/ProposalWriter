# Abstract — FAAM-ENI Circular Energy
## INNOVFUND-2025-NZT-CLEAN-TECH-MANUFACTURING

---

Europe sources >99% of its lithium iron phosphate (LFP) cathode active material (CAM) from China — a dependency that China's 2025 export controls on battery technology have transformed into an acute supply security threat. FAAM-ENI Circular Energy will establish the first LFP CAM production plant in Italy and Southern Europe — 50 kton/yr across 4 modular lines — at Eni's brownfield industrial site in Brindisi (Puglia), integrating three innovations: (1) progressively electrified process heat (66.6% electrification at entry into operation) — electric roller hearth kiln calcination (650-700 °C, 95% thermal efficiency vs 25-60% for gas kilns) and heat pump-assisted spray drying, with a roadmap to >95% electrification; (2) the first ISO 23247-compliant digital twin for CAM powder synthesis, deploying a multi-scale physics-based modelling framework with closed-loop model-predictive control; and (3) the first formal Quality-by-Design/PAT framework for LFP manufacturing, linking Critical Quality Attributes (D50 0.7-2.5 µm, >98% olivine purity, >=155 mAh/g) to Critical Process Parameters in real time. GHG avoidance is calculated on the use phase of manufactured battery components per Innovation Fund methodology Section 1.3.4. The digital twin targets 10-50% scrap reduction, 12-15% energy savings, and >40% batch-to-batch variability reduction. Financial close within 24 months, entry into operation within 48 months, leveraging FIB's 50+ years of battery manufacturing expertise and Eni's site and energy infrastructure. Directly addresses C/2025/3236 and generates EU-owned digital manufacturing IP.
