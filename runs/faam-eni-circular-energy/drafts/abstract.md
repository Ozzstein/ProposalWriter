# Abstract — FAAM-ENI Circular Energy
## INNOVFUND-2025-NZT-CLEAN-TECH-MANUFACTURING

---

Europe sources >99% of its lithium iron phosphate (LFP) cathode active material (CAM) from China — a dependency that China's 2025 export controls on battery technology have transformed into an acute supply security threat. FAAM-ENI Circular Energy will establish the first LFP CAM production plant in Italy and Southern Europe, at Eni's brownfield industrial site in Brindisi (Puglia), integrating three breakthrough innovations: (1) fully electrified process heat — electric roller hearth kiln calcination (650-700 °C, 95% thermal efficiency vs 25-60% for gas kilns) and heat pump-assisted spray drying, eliminating all Scope 1 combustion emissions; (2) the first ISO 23247-compliant digital twin for CAM powder synthesis, deploying a multi-scale physics-based modelling framework (DFT to plant-scale) with closed-loop model-predictive control; and (3) the first formal Quality-by-Design/PAT framework for LFP manufacturing, linking Critical Quality Attributes (D50 1-3 µm, >98% olivine purity, >155 mAh/g capacity) to Critical Process Parameters in real time. Relative GHG avoidance against the gas-fired Chinese benchmark reaches 55-70% by 2030 (aligned with Italy's NECP grid decarbonisation trajectory) and 80-90% by 2035. The digital twin is expected to deliver 10-50% scrap reduction, 12-15% energy savings, and >40% reduction in batch-to-batch variability. The project reaches financial close within 24 months and enters operation within 48 months of grant agreement signature, leveraging FIB's battery manufacturing expertise and Eni's site, process engineering capability, and energy infrastructure. It directly addresses Commission Communication C/2025/3236, delivers the first EU-manufactured, electrified LFP CAM, and generates EU-owned digital manufacturing IP transferable across the battery materials value chain.
