# Abstract — FAAM-ENI Circular Energy
## INNOVFUND-2025-NZT-CLEAN-TECH-MANUFACTURING

---

Europe sources >99% of its lithium iron phosphate (LFP) cathode active material (CAM) from China — a dependency that China's 2025 export controls have transformed into an acute supply threat. FAAM-ENI Circular Energy will establish a vertically integrated clean energy manufacturing hub at Eni's brownfield site in Brindisi (Puglia): a 50 kton/yr LFP CAM plant feeding a co-located FIB-Eni JV battery cell and BESS factory, with Eni deploying the BESS on the Italian grid — creating an end-to-end European value chain from cathode material to grid stabilisation. The CAM plant integrates three innovations: (1) progressively electrified process heat (66.6% at entry into operation) — electric roller hearth kiln calcination (650-700 C, 95% vs 25-60% thermal efficiency) and heat pump-assisted spray drying, roadmap to >95%; (2) the first ISO 23247-compliant digital twin for CAM synthesis with closed-loop model-predictive control; (3) the first Quality-by-Design/PAT framework for LFP manufacturing. GHG avoidance is classified under ES/Manufacturing of Components: calculated on the BESS use phase displacing fossil peakers on the Italian grid. Eni as guaranteed internal offtaker eliminates market risk. Digital twin targets 10-50% scrap reduction, 12-15% energy savings, >40% variability reduction. Financial close within 24 months, entry into operation within 48 months. Directly addresses C/2025/3236 and delivers full EU battery value chain sovereignty.
