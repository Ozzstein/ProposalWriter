# NSF Proposal Outline

## Proposal Structure

### 1. Project Summary (1 page)
- Overview
- Intellectual Merit
- Broader Impacts

### 2. Project Description (15 pages)
- Introduction and background
- Proposed research
  - Research objectives
  - Methods and approaches
  - Expected results
- Broader impacts
- Results from prior NSF support (if applicable)
- Timeline

### 3. References Cited (no page limit)

### 4. Additional Components
- Budget Justification
- Facilities, Equipment and Other Resources
- Biographical Sketch(es)
- Data Management Plan
- Mentoring Plan (if applicable)

## Formatting Requirements
- Font: 10pt minimum (Computer Modern, Arial, Courier New, Helvetica, Palatino, Times New Roman)
- Margins: 1 inch all sides
- Single-spaced
- Project Description: 15 pages maximum

## Review Criteria
- **Intellectual Merit**: Potential to advance knowledge
- **Broader Impacts**: Potential to benefit society and contribute to desired outcomes

## Tips
- NSF emphasizes broader impacts equally with intellectual merit
- Be specific about how broader impacts will be achieved and assessed
- Include a clear management plan for multi-PI projects
