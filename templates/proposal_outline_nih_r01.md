# NIH R01 Proposal Outline

## Proposal Structure

### 1. Specific Aims (1 page)
- Opening paragraph: significance of the problem
- Gap statement: what is unknown or unsolved
- Long-term goal and objective of this application
- Central hypothesis and rationale
- Specific Aim 1: [title]
  - Working hypothesis
  - Approach summary
  - Expected outcome
- Specific Aim 2: [title]
  - Working hypothesis
  - Approach summary
  - Expected outcome
- Specific Aim 3: [title] (if applicable)
- Payoff paragraph: how success will advance the field

### 2. Research Strategy (12 pages)

#### 2a. Significance (2-3 pages)
- Importance of the problem
- Critical barriers to progress
- How this project will improve scientific knowledge or clinical practice
- Relevance to NIH mission

#### 2b. Innovation (1-2 pages)
- Novel theoretical concepts or approaches
- Novel methodologies or technologies
- Refinement of existing approaches

#### 2c. Approach (7-8 pages)
- **Preliminary data** (if available)
- **Aim 1**: Rationale, experimental design, methods, expected outcomes, potential problems and alternatives
- **Aim 2**: Same structure
- **Aim 3**: Same structure (if applicable)
- **Timeline and milestones**
- **Rigor and reproducibility**

### 3. Bibliography / References Cited (no page limit)

### 4. Additional Components
- Facilities & Other Resources
- Equipment
- Budget Justification
- Biosketches
- Data Management and Sharing Plan

## Formatting Requirements
- Font: Arial 11pt or equivalent
- Margins: 0.5 inches minimum
- Single-spaced
- Page limits are strictly enforced
- Specific Aims: 1 page
- Research Strategy: 12 pages total

## Scoring Criteria (NIH Standard)
- **Significance** (scored 1-9): Does the project address an important problem?
- **Investigator(s)** (scored 1-9): Are they well-suited?
- **Innovation** (scored 1-9): Does it shift current paradigms?
- **Approach** (scored 1-9): Are the methods well-reasoned?
- **Environment** (scored 1-9): Is the scientific environment appropriate?
- **Overall Impact** (scored 1-9): Composite assessment

## Tips for NIH Reviewers
- Reviewers are experts but may not be in your exact subfield
- Be explicit about what is novel — don't assume they'll see it
- Address potential weaknesses proactively
- Quantify impact where possible
- Use clear headings and structure — reviewers read many proposals
