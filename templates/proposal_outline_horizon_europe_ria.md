# Horizon Europe — Research & Innovation Action (RIA) / Innovation Action (IA)
# Part B Application Template

> **IMPORTANT**: This is a reference template based on the standard Horizon Europe Part B structure.
> If the user has uploaded an official Part B template for the specific call, use that instead —
> it takes precedence over this file. Check `runs/{project}/inputs/call_template.*` first.

## Overview

| Item | Details |
|---|---|
| Instrument | RIA (100% funding) / IA (70% for profit entities, 100% for non-profit) |
| Part B page limit | **70 pages** (Sections 1–3 combined) |
| Evaluation criteria | Excellence / Impact / Implementation — each scored 0–5 |
| Threshold per criterion | 3.0 / 5.0 |
| Overall threshold | 10.0 / 15.0 |
| Submission portal | EU Funding & Tenders Portal |

## Part B Structure

### Section 1 — Excellence
*Recommended length: ~28–30 pages*

**1.1 Objectives and ambition**
- Description of objectives (clear, measurable, achievable within the project)
- Relation to the work programme: how the project addresses the call topic
- The challenge or problem being addressed
- Why this challenge is important now

**1.2 Relation to the work programme**
- Specific expected outcomes from the call that this project targets
- How the project contributes to each expected outcome
- Alignment with EU policy priorities (Green Deal, Digital Decade, etc.)

**1.3 Concept and approach**
- Overall concept and the hypothesis or key idea
- Current state of the art — what exists, what its limitations are
- How your approach goes beyond the state of the art
- Key innovations and their novelty
- Interdisciplinary or cross-sectoral elements (if applicable)

**1.4 Ambition**
- What breakthroughs or step-changes are targeted
- How success would change the field
- Gender dimension: how sex and/or gender is considered in the research content (mandatory if applicable)

---

### Section 2 — Impact
*Recommended length: ~20 pages*

**2.1 Expected outcomes and impacts**
- Short-term outcomes (during the project)
- Medium/long-term impacts (after the project ends)
- Contribution to the specific expected outcomes listed in the call
- Key Performance Indicators (KPIs) with baseline and target values
- Pathways to impact: how outputs become outcomes, how outcomes lead to impacts
- Wider societal, economic, and environmental impacts

**2.2 Measures to maximise impact**

*2.2.1 Dissemination and exploitation of results*
- Open access publication plan (mandatory: publications must be open access)
- Data management plan summary (full DMP as deliverable D1.x)
- Exploitation routes: commercialisation, licensing, standards, policy uptake
- IP management: ownership, protection strategy, freedom to operate
- Knowledge transfer mechanisms

*2.2.2 Communication and outreach*
- Target audiences beyond the scientific community
- Communication activities and channels
- Engagement with policymakers, industry, civil society

---

### Section 3 — Implementation
*Recommended length: ~20 pages*

**3.1 Work plan — work packages, deliverables, and milestones**

*3.1.1 Overview of the work plan*
- Gantt chart or PERT diagram
- Summary table of all work packages

*3.1.2 Work package descriptions* (one subsection per WP)
For each work package:
- WP number, title, lead partner, contributing partners
- Objectives
- Description of work and methodology (tasks with task leaders)
- Deliverables (D number, title, type, dissemination level, due month)
- Milestones (MS number, title, means of verification, due month)

**Standard WPs:**
- WP1: Project Management and Coordination
- WP2–WPn: Research/Technical work packages
- WPn+1: Dissemination, Exploitation, and Communication (DEC)
- WPn+2: Ethics requirements (if applicable)

**3.2 Management structure, milestones, and procedures**
- Organisational structure and decision-making bodies (General Assembly, Steering Committee, etc.)
- Project Coordinator role and responsibilities
- Quality assurance and risk management procedures
- Risk table: risk description, likelihood (L/M/H), impact (L/M/H), mitigation measure
- Data management plan summary
- Open science practices

**3.3 Consortium as a whole**
- Complementarity of partners (why this specific consortium)
- Previous collaboration history
- How the consortium covers the necessary expertise and resources
- Geographic balance (if relevant to the call)
- Third-party involvement and subcontracting (if applicable)

**3.4 Resources and cost overview**
- Total budget summary table (by WP and by partner)
- Budget justification narrative
  - Personnel costs (explain FTEs per WP)
  - Subcontracting (justify why and what)
  - Travel and subsistence
  - Other direct costs (equipment, consumables, etc.)
- In-kind contributions (if applicable)
- Financial capacity of partners

---

## Annexes (not counted in the 70-page limit)

- **CVs**: Max 2 pages per key team member
- **Activity reports**: For established legal entities, last 2 years (max 2 pages)
- **Third-party entities**: Description if not part of consortium (if applicable)
- **Ethics self-assessment**: Pre-screening table (completed online in the portal)
- **Letters of commitment**: From associated partners or third parties (if required by call)

## Evaluation Scoring Guide

| Score | Meaning |
|---|---|
| 0 | Proposal fails to address the criterion or cannot be assessed |
| 1 | Poor — criterion addressed in an inadequate manner |
| 2 | Fair — criterion addressed but with significant weaknesses |
| 3 | Good — criterion well addressed but with some shortcomings (**threshold**) |
| 4 | Very good — criterion well addressed with minor shortcomings |
| 5 | Excellent — criterion fully addressed with no shortcomings |

Each criterion (Excellence, Impact, Implementation) is scored independently by each evaluator. Scores are averaged across the panel.

---

## Key Tips for HE Evaluators

- **Excellence**: Evaluators look for clear novelty beyond SOTA, precise objectives, and a convincing methodology. Vague claims of novelty fail.
- **Impact**: Must link explicitly to the expected outcomes listed in the work programme. Generic impact claims are penalised.
- **Implementation**: Work plans must be realistic. Overloaded WP leaders and missing risk mitigation are common failure points.
- **Gender dimension**: If the research involves human subjects or biological material, gender must be addressed or explicitly justified as not applicable.
- **Open science**: Open access and data management are mandatory, not optional.
